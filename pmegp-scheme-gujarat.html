Prime Minister's Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP)

Empowering micro-enterprises across Gujarat to kickstart business dreams.
üìã Scheme Overview
The Prime Minister's Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP) is a credit-linked subsidy scheme launched by the Government of India in August 2008. It emerged from the merger of two previous schemes: the Prime Minister's Rojgar Yojana (PMRY) and the Rural Employment Generation Programme (REGP).

Current Status: The scheme has been approved for continuation for five years, from FY 2021-22 to FY 2025-26, with some modifications to its existing guidelines. The online portal for PMEGP became operational with the revised guidelines from June 1, 2022.

Aim: To create employment opportunities by facilitating the establishment of micro-enterprises in both rural and urban non-farm sectors.
üéØ Objectives
  • To generate employment opportunities in rural and urban areas by supporting new self-employment ventures/projects/micro enterprises.
  • To bring together traditional artisans and unemployed youth from both rural and urban areas, providing them with self-employment opportunities.
  • To ensure continuous and sustainable employment for a large segment of traditional and prospective artisans, and rural and urban unemployed youth, thereby helping to curb rural-to-urban migration.
  • To increase the wage-earning capacity of workers and artisans, contributing to the growth of rural and urban employment.
üõ†Ô∏è Implementing Agencies
National Level: The PMEGP is a central sector scheme administered by the Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MoMSME). Khadi and Village Industries Commission (KVIC) acts as the single nodal agency.

State Level: The scheme is implemented through:
  • State offices of KVIC
  • State Khadi and Village Industries Boards (KVIBs)
  • District Industries Centres (DICs)
  • Coir Board (for coir-related activities)
  • Banks
Other Agencies: National Scheduled Castes Finance and Development Corporation (NSCFDC), National Scheduled Tribes Finance and Development Corporation (NSTFDC), and various Entrepreneurship Development Institutes may also be involved.
‚úÖ Eligibility Conditions
For New Micro Enterprises:
  • Any individual above 18 years of age.
  • No income ceiling for assistance.
  • For projects costing above ‚Çπ10 lakh in manufacturing or above ‚Çπ5 lakh in business/service sectors, beneficiaries must have at least an VIII standard pass educational qualification.
  • Assistance is only available for new projects sanctioned under PMEGP.
  • Existing units that have already availed government subsidy under any other scheme are not eligible.
  • Projects without Capital Expenditure are not eligible.
  • Cost of land should not be included in the project cost, but the cost of ready-built shed or long-term lease/rental workshop for up to 3 years can be included.
  • Trading activities are generally restricted but permitted with specific conditions in NER, LWE-affected districts, A&N Islands, or for selling Khadi/Village Industry products.
  • Transport activities are allowed, but a ceiling of 10% on financed projects applies in most areas.
  • Only one person from one family (self and spouse) is eligible.
For Upgradation of Existing PMEGP/REGP/MUDRA Units:
  • Margin Money (subsidy) claimed under PMEGP must have been successfully adjusted after a lock-in period of 3 years.
  • The first loan under PMEGP/REGP/MUDRA must have been successfully repaid.
  • The unit should be profitable with good turnover and potential for growth through modernization/upgrading technology.
üí∞ Financial Assistance (Margin Money Subsidy)
The financial assistance is provided as a Margin Money Subsidy based on the project cost. The balance amount (excluding own contribution) is provided by banks.

üè¶ Bank Finance
  • Banks will sanction 90% of the project cost for General Category beneficiaries and 95% for Special Category beneficiaries, disbursing the full amount for project setup.
  • Financing can be in the form of term loan for Capital Expenditure and cash credit for Working Capital, or as a composite loan.
üìä Training & Other Support
Entrepreneurship Development Programme (EDP) Training: Mandatory training is provided to beneficiaries. This can be offline or through virtual classrooms, conducted by various recognized training centers.

Backward and Forward Linkages: Funds are earmarked for activities like awareness camps, workshops, exhibitions, publicity, physical verification, and marketing support, including through KVIC's sales outlets and participation in trade fairs.

Physical Verification: 100% physical verification with geo-tagging of established units is mandatory and conducted by KVIC through third-party agencies.
